# LifeMate AI Revenue Model

## Revenue Philosophy

LifeMate AI should be accessible to low-income and underserved users while still building a sustainable business. The best model is blended: free essential access, paid premium value, and institutional sponsorship.

## Pricing Tiers

| Plan | Target User | Monthly Price | Features |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Free | Individual users | $0 | Basic assistant, local reminders, limited translation, limited summaries |
| Plus | Students/professionals | $3-$8 region-adjusted | More AI usage, document summaries, planning, career guidance, budget tools |
| Family | Caregivers/families | $8-$20 | Family dashboard, alerts, multiple profiles, caregiver summaries |
| Clinic/School | Institutions | Custom | Admin dashboard, onboarding, analytics, bulk accounts |
| Government/NGO | Public deployments | Custom | Regional language packs, scheme database, reporting, offline distribution |
| API | Partners | Usage-based | Scheme guidance, document summarization, multilingual assistant modules |

## Unit Economics Example

Assumptions for a paid individual user:

- Monthly subscription: $5
- AI and infrastructure cost: $0.80-$1.50
- Notification cost: $0.10-$0.40
- Support allocation: $0.30
- Gross margin target: 60%-75%

Family plan improves economics because multiple users share dashboard infrastructure and retention is stronger.

## Ethical Monetization Rules

LifeMate AI should not exploit vulnerable users.

Rules:

- No predatory loan referrals.
- No selling private health or wellness data.
- No dark patterns around emergencies.
- Ads should not appear in crisis, health, or SOS workflows.
- Marketplace recommendations must be clearly labeled and optional.

## Best Initial Revenue Wedge

Start with Family Plan:

- Clear buyer: adult children or caregivers
- Strong emotional value
- Direct link to medicine reminders and SOS
- Higher willingness to pay
- Better retention

Second wedge:

Institution pilots with clinics, universities, and NGOs.
